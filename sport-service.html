Sports Rehabilitation

At Aura Physiotherapy and Rehab Centre, we provide comprehensive care for sports injuries — from the first signs of pain to full return to sport. Whether you’re a professional athlete or a weekend enthusiast, our sports rehab programs are tailored to help you recover faster, move better, and prevent re-injury.

We focus not just on healing the injury but also on restoring strength, flexibility, and performance through evidence-based physiotherapy and functional training.

Sprains and Strains

These soft tissue injuries are common in sports and involve overstretched or torn ligaments (sprains) or muscles/tendons (strains). We treat these with a progressive rehabilitation plan that includes pain relief, swelling reduction, gentle mobilization, and strengthening — ensuring safe return to activity.

Fractures

Recovery from a sports-related fracture requires more than bone healing. Our rehab includes post-immobilization physiotherapy, joint mobilization, muscle reactivation, and functional movement retraining to help athletes regain confidence and control in the injured area.

Tendinopathy

Tendon injuries — such as Achilles tendinitis or tennis elbow — result from repetitive strain or overload. We provide eccentric strengthening exercises, soft tissue therapy, ultrasound treatment, and activity modification guidance to promote healing and restore tendon resilience.

Knee Injuries

From ACL tears to meniscal injuries, knee problems are among the most common in sports. At Aura, we offer post-injury and post-surgical rehab with a focus on restoring joint mobility, quadriceps and hamstring strength, stability, and proper biomechanics for sports performance.

Shoulder Injuries

Rotator cuff strains, dislocations, and impingements can restrict shoulder function. Our shoulder rehab focuses on scapular stability, rotator cuff strengthening, posture correction, and sports-specific training to ensure full pain-free range and throwing or lifting ability.

Overuse Injuries

Conditions like shin splints, stress fractures, or runner’s knee occur from repetitive loading. Our treatment addresses faulty movement patterns, load management, gait correction, and muscle balance to not only heal the injury but prevent it from recurring.

Concussions

Sports-related concussions require careful management. Our post-concussion rehab includes graded return-to-play protocols, balance and coordination training, visual-motor exercises, and symptom monitoring — all in coordination with medical professionals.

Post-Surgical Rehabilitation

After orthopaedic sports surgeries (e.g., ACL reconstruction, shoulder labral repair), rehab plays a crucial role in recovery. We follow structured protocols involving pain control, mobility restoration, neuromuscular training, and strength conditioning — progressing step by step toward full athletic readiness.

Chronic Sports Conditions

For athletes dealing with persistent pain or recurring injuries, we offer long-term rehab strategies to correct underlying biomechanical issues, improve movement efficiency, and restore pain-free function through strength, mobility, and control training.

Restoring Range of Motion

Inflexibility or restricted joint movement can limit performance and increase injury risk. We focus on joint mobilizations, myofascial release, and active stretching techniques to restore complete, functional range of motion relevant to your sport or activity.

Strengthening & Functional Training

The final phase of rehab is critical — preparing the body for the physical demands of your sport. We offer sport-specific strengthening, agility drills, plyometrics, and neuromuscular training to bridge the gap between rehab and performance, ensuring a confident and complete return to play.
