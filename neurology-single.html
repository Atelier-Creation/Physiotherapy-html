Neurology

At Aura Physiotherapy and Rehab Centre, we specialize in neurological rehabilitation for patients affected by disorders of the brain, spinal cord, and peripheral nerves. Our programs are carefully designed to promote functional recovery, improve independence, and enhance quality of life through targeted physiotherapy and neuro-rehabilitation techniques.

We understand that neurological conditions affect both movement and confidence — our goal is to restore both through consistent, compassionate care.

Spinal Cord Injury

A spinal cord injury can cause partial or complete loss of movement and sensation. At Aura, we provide intensive rehabilitation to improve muscle strength, mobility, and nerve function. Our approach includes gait training, wheelchair mobility, spasticity management, and re-education of motor control.

Traumatic Brain Injury (TBI)

Patients recovering from TBI often face physical, cognitive, and behavioral challenges. Our therapy focuses on improving balance, coordination, strength, and motor planning. We work on restoring daily activity function while supporting neuroplasticity and mental engagement through structured tasks.

Transverse Myelitis

This rare inflammation of the spinal cord can lead to sudden muscle weakness or paralysis. Our treatment emphasizes progressive mobility exercises, gait training, and strengthening to help patients regain independence as inflammation subsides and nerve signals improve.

Fibromyalgia

A chronic condition causing widespread pain, fatigue, and sleep issues. Our physiotherapy program includes gentle stretching, aerobic conditioning, postural correction, and relaxation techniques — all aimed at reducing pain sensitivity and improving quality of life.

Stroke Rehabilitation

After a stroke, many patients experience paralysis, balance problems, and speech difficulties. At Aura, our stroke rehab focuses on retraining lost movements, regaining muscle control, improving balance, and restoring independence in daily activities. We also offer gait training and upper limb re-education

Parkinson’s Disease

Parkinson’s causes tremors, stiffness, and slow movements. Our rehabilitation focuses on improving mobility, posture, balance, and coordination through specialized exercises like LSVT-based techniques. We also train patients to overcome freezing episodes and maintain functional independence.

Guillain-Barré Syndrome (GBS)

GBS can lead to rapid-onset muscle weakness due to nerve inflammation. Once medically stable, our therapy aids in strengthening weakened muscles, improving mobility, and managing fatigue. The focus is on gradual recovery of function and rebuilding endurance.

Facial Palsy (Bell’s Palsy)

Facial paralysis affects daily expressions and confidence. We provide targeted facial exercises, neuromuscular re-education, and electrical stimulation to support nerve recovery and facial symmetry restoration.

Peripheral Neuropathy

This condition causes tingling, numbness, or weakness in the limbs due to nerve damage. Our treatment includes balance training, coordination drills, and foot care education, with a strong emphasis on improving walking stability and preventing falls.

Multiple Sclerosis (MS)

MS affects the brain and spinal cord, causing symptoms like fatigue, weakness, and coordination issues. Our MS rehabilitation program combines muscle strengthening, energy conservation techniques, balance training, and stretching to manage symptoms and maintain function.

Balance & Coordination Disorders

We assess and treat balance impairments resulting from neurological dysfunctions using vestibular training, proprioceptive exercises, and coordination drills. Our therapy helps prevent falls and promotes confident, stable movement in daily life.

Myasthenia Gravis

A neuromuscular disorder that causes weakness in voluntary muscles. We provide energy-efficient exercise plans, postural control, breathing techniques, and mobility support, focusing on managing fatigue and enhancing physical function safely.

Polyneuropathy

Polyneuropathy involves damage to multiple peripheral nerves, leading to weakness, tingling, or numbness in both hands and feet. Our rehab focuses on improving walking ability, balance, and limb coordination, while also guiding foot care and protective strategies.

Amyotrophic Lateral Sclerosis (ALS)

ALS is a progressive disease that leads to muscle weakness and mobility challenges. Though there is no cure, our physiotherapy aims to preserve mobility as long as possible, reduce stiffness, and improve respiratory function through a multidisciplinary care approach.
