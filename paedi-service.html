Paediatrics

At Aura Physiotherapy and Rehab Centre, we offer compassionate and specialized paediatric physiotherapy for children with developmental, neurological, and genetic conditions. Our goal is to support every child in achieving their fullest potential — physically, emotionally, and socially.

Our treatments are based on play-based, goal-oriented therapy plans that encourage movement, independence, and confidence. Whether your child needs early intervention or ongoing support, we are here to walk the journey with you.

Cerebral Palsy (CP)

Cerebral palsy affects muscle tone, movement, and posture due to brain damage before, during, or shortly after birth. Our CP rehabilitation includes posture correction, strengthening, gait training, and developmental therapy. We use assistive equipment and neurodevelopmental techniques to help children gain independence in movement.

Developmental Delay

If a child is slower to reach milestones like sitting, walking, or talking, early physiotherapy can make a major difference. At Aura, we assess and treat gross motor delays using age-appropriate exercises, sensory integration, and parent-guided training plans to support healthy development.

Spina Bifida

Children born with spina bifida may have difficulty with walking, posture, or bladder control. We offer therapeutic exercises, orthotic support, and balance training to improve mobility and prevent deformities. Our focus is on maximizing independence and daily function.

Genetic Conditions

Children with genetic disorders often experience motor difficulties. We provide personalized therapy for conditions like Rett syndrome, Angelman syndrome, or Fragile X — supporting physical development, coordination, and posture through consistent physiotherapy and caregiver education.

Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD)

Children with autism may face challenges in coordination, body awareness, and motor planning. Our sensory-motor integration therapy uses play-based techniques to improve attention, balance, and motor skills while ensuring a comfortable and encouraging environment for every child.

Hydrocephalus

Hydrocephalus (fluid build-up in the brain) can affect balance and movement. After medical management (e.g., shunt), our therapy helps in regaining motor control, coordination, and head posture, while addressing any developmental delay

Encephalopathy

Brain injuries in infancy or childhood (due to oxygen loss, infection, or trauma) can result in movement impairments. We focus on muscle strengthening, spasticity management, and neuro-motor retraining to help children regain functional abilities over time.

Muscular Dystrophy

A group of inherited disorders that cause progressive muscle weakness. Our goal is to maintain function for as long as possible through gentle strengthening, mobility exercises, breathing support, and assistive device training.

Spinal Muscular Atrophy (SMA)

SMA affects motor nerve cells and leads to muscle wasting. At Aura, we provide respiratory physiotherapy, limb mobility exercises, and supportive positioning to improve comfort, posture, and physical interaction in daily life.

Down Syndrome

Children with Down syndrome often have low muscle tone and delayed motor milestones. We offer early physiotherapy that focuses on improving balance, walking patterns, and strength — using playful and positive reinforcement-based approaches.

Acquired Brain Injury (ABI)

Injuries to the brain after birth (accidents, infections, strokes) can disrupt development. We provide structured rehabilitation programs to address balance, strength, coordination, and cognitive-motor integration to help your child regain lost skills.

Cognitive Therapy

Children with cognitive or learning difficulties benefit from targeted therapy to improve attention, problem-solving, memory, and motor planning. Our sessions are interactive, engaging, and designed to support both brain and body development.

ADHD (Hyperactivity)

Children with ADHD often show restlessness, impulsivity, and poor coordination. Our physiotherapy approach includes focus-building games, balance tasks, sensory regulation techniques, and structured movement routines to channel their energy positively.

Torticollis

A condition where the child tilts their head to one side due to tight neck muscles. We use gentle stretching, positioning, and parent education to correct posture early and ensure proper head and spine alignment.

Macrocephaly / Microcephaly

Abnormal head sizes can impact balance and development. We provide customized physiotherapy focusing on posture control, motor skills, and sensory-motor integration for better coordination and safety

Ataxia

Ataxia affects balance and muscle coordination, leading to unsteady movements. We provide gait training, balance exercises, and coordination drills to enhance body control and reduce fall risks during play and daily routines.
