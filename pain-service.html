Pain Management

At Aura Physiotherapy and Rehab Centre, our Pain Management program is designed to provide long-term relief from a wide range of acute and chronic pain conditions. Whether your discomfort is caused by injury, posture, neurological issues, or autoimmune disorders — our evidence-based, non-invasive therapies focus on restoring comfort, mobility, and quality of life.

Our approach includes advanced physiotherapy, posture correction, therapeutic exercises, manual therapy, and modalities like ultrasound, TENS, and dry needling — tailored to your pain type and root cause.

Fracture Rehabilitation

Fractures don’t just heal with a cast — they need guided recovery. At Aura, we help patients regain strength, mobility, and confidence after a fracture. Our program includes pain management, joint movement exercises, muscle reconditioning, and gradual reintegration into daily activities.

Post-Surgical Rehabilitation

After orthopaedic surgeries like joint replacements, fracture fixations, or ligament reconstructions, physiotherapy is essential. We provide structured rehabilitation plans that help reduce post-operative pain and swelling, improve joint mobility, and rebuild muscle strength — aligned with your surgeon’s protocol.

Frozen Shoulder

Frozen shoulder causes severe stiffness and limited movement in the shoulder joint. Our treatment focuses on restoring range through joint mobilization, stretching, manual therapy, and gradual strengthening — helping you return to normal use without discomfort.

Osteoarthritis & Rheumatoid Arthritis

Whether caused by wear and tear (osteoarthritis) or autoimmune inflammation (rheumatoid arthritis), these conditions can cause joint pain, swelling, and stiffness. At Aura, we use personalized therapy to improve joint function, reduce inflammation, and maintain your mobility through safe, guided exercises.

Cervical Spondylosis

Age-related degeneration in the neck spine can lead to persistent neck pain and stiffness. Our cervical spondylosis therapy includes postural correction, neck mobility restoration, strengthening of cervical muscles, and pain-relieving modalities such as TENS and ultrasound.

Lumbar Spondylosis

Back pain caused by lumbar degeneration is addressed through core stabilization, postural correction, spinal mobility training, and strengthening exercises. Our goal is to improve daily function and prevent further progression.

Spondylolisthesis (Cervical or Lumbar)

Spondylolisthesis, where one vertebra slips over another, can cause instability and nerve pain. We offer physiotherapy programs focused on spinal alignment, strengthening deep core muscles, and maintaining safe postures during movement.

Sciatica

Sciatica results in sharp pain radiating from the lower back down the leg, due to sciatic nerve compression. We use nerve mobilization, targeted stretches, postural correction, and core stability exercises to relieve symptoms and prevent recurrence.

Disc Bulge / Herniated Disc

A slipped or bulging disc can compress spinal nerves, leading to pain, numbness, or tingling. At Aura, we follow evidence-based practices like the McKenzie method, spinal decompression techniques, and strengthening therapy to relieve symptoms and stabilize your spine.

Total Knee / Hip Replacement Rehab

Rehabilitation after joint replacement is vital for long-term success. Our team provides post-operative therapy focused on improving joint flexibility, walking patterns, strength, and pain control. We guide you step-by-step toward independent mobility

Vertigo (BPPV)

Benign Paroxysmal Positional Vertigo (BPPV) causes sudden dizziness due to inner ear dysfunction. Our vestibular physiotherapy includes repositioning maneuvers like the Epley technique, balance retraining, and desensitization exercises for long-term relief.

Osteoporosis

Osteoporosis weakens bones and increases the risk of fractures. Our physiotherapy helps strengthen muscles and improve balance to prevent falls. We also offer education on safe movement and guided exercise routines to support bone health.

Calcaneal Spur (Heel Spur)

Heel pain from calcaneal spur is treated through ultrasound therapy, plantar fascia stretching, heel pad recommendations, and foot posture correction. We aim to reduce inflammation, ease walking pain, and prevent recurrence.
